Our Story

Born from Grandma's Kitchen

How a dream in Maboneng became a movement across Johannesburg.

The Beginning

Ubuntu Eats was founded in 2018 by chef and entrepreneur Nandi Dlamini. Growing up in Soweto, Nandi spent her weekends in her grandmother Mama Dlamini's kitchen — a place filled with the aromas of slow-cooked samp, freshly ground spices, and laughter that spilled into the street.

After completing her culinary training in Cape Town and working in restaurants across East Africa, Nandi returned home with a clear vision: to build a restaurant that didn't just serve African food, but celebrated the stories, people, and traditions behind every dish.

In March 2018, Ubuntu Eats opened its first doors at 17 Fox Street in Maboneng Precinct — and has never looked back.

Our Journey

From one kitchen to three thriving locations.

2018

The First Table

Ubuntu Eats opens in Maboneng Precinct. Fully booked within the first two weeks.

2019

Catering Division Launched

Growing demand from corporate clients led to the launch of our dedicated catering service.

2021

Sandton Opens

Ubuntu Eats expands to Sandton City, bringing African cuisine to the heart of Johannesburg's business district.

2023

Soweto Homecoming

The Soweto branch opens — a deeply personal milestone for founder Nandi, returning to her roots.

2026

Digital Transformation

New website launch with online reservations, ordering, and loyalty rewards programme.
